# Quality-of-Earnings Readiness Program

**Roadmap phase:** Phase 2 (6-18mo)

**Value impact:** Prevents a leading post-LOI price reduction — buyer-driven reversal of addbacks that were not supported when the LOI was signed — by surfacing and fixing the same findings first.

## Why buyers care

Above roughly **$1-2M of EBITDA**, buyers commission an independent **Quality of Earnings
(QoE)** review that normalizes EBITDA, tests revenue, examines working capital, and
challenges every addback. Findings discovered *after* the LOI become leverage to retrade
the price. A sell-side QoE — running the buyer's playbook on yourself first — converts
those surprises into a prepared, defensible position and shortens diligence.

## How to run it

1. Reconcile the financials and lock a clean trial balance the numbers tie back to.
2. Rebuild the normalized-EBITDA bridge with a source document behind every addback, and
   rate each addback's challenge likelihood (low / medium / high / not defensible).
3. Test revenue recognition on a sample of transactions the way a buyer's accountants
   will.
4. Commission a sell-side QoE (or simulate one with the advisor) and remediate findings
   while they are still cheap to fix — before going to market.

## Evidence this produces

- A reconciled statement set tied to the trial balance.
- A normalized-EBITDA bridge with support and a challenge-likelihood rating per addback.
- A revenue-recognition test memo and a remediation log.
