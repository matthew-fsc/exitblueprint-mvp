# Customer Diversification Program

**Roadmap phase:** Phase 2 (6-18mo)

**Value impact:** EBITDA x 0.50 multiple expansion typical (DRS +15-20 pts if top customer currently >35%).

## Tasks

| seq | title | owner_role | offset_days |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Build concentration heatmap; identify Tier 1 (>10%) accounts | advisor | 14 |
| 2 | Secure multi-year renewals on Tier 1 accounts; remove change-of-control clauses where possible | owner | 90 |
| 3 | Launch targeted acquisition plan for accounts in underweighted segments | owner | 120 |
| 4 | Institutionalize Tier 1 relationships: second point of contact, account team | ops | 150 |
| 5 | Track top-1 and top-5 share monthly against reduction targets | advisor | 180 |

## Why buyers care

Customer concentration is one of the most-watched risks in diligence. As a rough guide:
under 10% of revenue from one customer is clean; 10-20% draws questions; 20-30% is a yellow
flag; over 30% and many buyers decline; over 40% is near-uninvestable on conventional terms.
Concentration above threshold commonly costs roughly 20-35% of value or pushes risk into the
structure (earnouts, holdbacks). Pre-empting it protects the multiple.

## Evidence this produces

- A concentration heatmap with Tier 1 (>10%) accounts identified.
- Multi-year renewals on Tier 1 accounts, with change-of-control terms addressed.
- A named-account diversification pipeline and monthly top-1 / top-5 tracking.
