# 00 - Product Brief

## Problem

Lower middle market owners considering an exit (1) don't have a deal yet, (2) struggle with low-level operational and financial issues that quietly destroy valuation, and (3) don't understand the exit prep process. Existing AI diligence tooling (Rogo, buy-side automation) serves institutions with deals in motion and expensive data subscriptions. Nothing serves the pre-deal readiness window.

## Product

A longitudinal readiness system sold through M&A advisor firms:

1. **Assess** - Advisor runs owner through a structured DRS assessment. Deterministic engine produces a versioned score with dimension breakdown.
2. **Diagnose** - Low scores map to named gaps ("customer concentration risk", "owner dependence").
3. **Prescribe** - Gaps map to remediation playbooks that become sequenced task roadmaps with owners and dates.
4. **Educate** - Content modules keyed to the client's specific gaps, dripped across the engagement.
5. **Re-assess** - Quarterly re-scoring; deltas show progress and keep the advisor relationship warm.

## Users

- **Advisor (primary, buys and drives the product):** manages a portfolio of client companies, runs assessments, reviews roadmaps, gets meeting-prep briefs.
- **Owner (secondary):** sees their score, roadmap, and education content via a portal (Phase 4).
- **Admin (Exit Blueprint):** manages firms, rubric versions, playbook library, content library.

## What compounds (protect these)

- The rubric + gap-to-playbook mapping (methodology IP)
- The longitudinal assessment dataset (scores over time, per company)
- Advisor distribution experience

## Success criteria for MVP (end of Phase 2)

An advisor can complete an intake with a client and hand them a professional scored readiness report the same day, with zero developer involvement.
